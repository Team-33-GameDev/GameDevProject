# Customer Meeting Notes (Asynchronous Review)

**Date:** June 14, 2026  
**Format:** Asynchronous Text Chat Review  
**Participants:** Customer (Mentor), Development Team  

### Context & Artifacts Presented
The team shared the `Concept.pdf` (Game Design Document for the "Wolf and Sheep" top-down stealth game) with the customer to review the initial User Stories, MoSCoW priorities, and MVP v1 scope for Week 2. 

### Customer Feedback & Discussion
The customer provided critical feedback regarding the market viability and target audience of the current concept:
1. **Platform Strategy:** Targeting Steam, Web, and Mobile simultaneously is unfeasible. Mobile is oversaturated, and Steam requires 2+ hours of gameplay with clear analogues. Web is the only viable platform for the current scope.
2. **Target Audience Mismatch:** The proposed TA (7-16 and 50+) does not align with the core emotions of the referenced games. The 50+ demographic does not typically play this genre, and the younger demographic expects different core loops.
3. **Product vs. Game Jam:** The current concept feels like a game jam project rather than a market-ready product. The team skipped market validation and went straight to implementation without a clear understanding of the audience.

### Team Proposal & Customer Approval (Deadline Adjustment)
Due to the approaching Week 2 deadline and the fact that the bulk of the current assignment was already completed based on the old concept, the team proposed the following to the customer:
*   **Proposal:** Finish and submit the current weekly assignment using the existing "Wolf and Sheep" concept to meet the immediate academic deadline. The team commits to completely pivoting and rethinking the project based on the customer's market requirements for the next assignment.
*   **Customer Decision:** **Approved.** The customer explicitly agreed ("Окей") to let the team submit the current work to satisfy the Week 2 requirements.
*   **Future Action Plan:** For Assignment 2, the team will select ONE clear, actual 2026 game reference and brainstorm a new concept tailored to a specific audience, summarized in a single pitch (e.g., "Horror chess" or "Co-op snipers roguelike"). The current MVP scope and User Stories remain unchanged for this specific Week 2 submission but will be completely rewritten in the next iteration.

### Consents & Administrative Notes
*   **MIT License Consent:** Written consent for the public MIT-licensed development model was explicitly obtained via chat prior to repository creation.
*   **Recording & Sharing:** As this was an asynchronous text review, no audio/video recording was made. The customer granted written permission to share these sanitized notes privately with the instructors for assessment. 
*   **Documentation:** The customer agreed that these detailed notes will serve as the official evidence of the review and approval of the Week 2 MVP scope in lieu of a live meeting transcript.
