# Week 7 Reflection (Sprint 5)

## Learning points
- **Follow-up Maintenance and Balancing**: We learned that in game development, "maintenance" often translates to numerical and logical balancing. Tweaking the factory DPS (production rate) and fixing the clicking phase input logic taught us that even minor adjustments to core loop variables require careful regression testing across the entire tension-release cycle to ensure the game doesn't become too slow or too chaotic.
- **Final Transition and Documentation as a Product**: Treating `docs/customer-handover.md`, `CONTRIBUTING.md`, and `AGENTS.md` as first-class deliverables shifted our perspective. We learned that writing for an end-user or a future maintainer requires a fundamentally different mindset than writing code; it forced us to abstract away our deep technical knowledge and focus purely on usability, setup, and operational clarity.
- **Stabilization for Demo Day**: Preparing for the final presentation taught us the discipline of a "feature freeze." We learned how to resist the urge to add "just one more small polish feature" and instead focus entirely on stabilizing `MVP v3`, ensuring that the build is reliable and reproducible for the 2-minute pre-recorded demo and the live Q&A.

## Validated assumptions
- **Architecture Decoupling Pays Off**: We assumed that decoupling our signals and game state (as documented in ADR-001) would make adding new environmental interactions straightforward. This was strongly validated during Sprint 5 when we implemented the crowbar interaction and the interactive door. Because the core game logic wasn't tightly coupled to the room elements, adding these new mechanics required minimal changes to the existing codebase.
- **Initial Gameplay Pacing**: We initially assumed that the baseline factory DPS and quota values from `MVP v2` would feel balanced. Through internal playtesting and trial runs, this assumption was rejected. The initial values made the preparation phase feel sluggish, confirming that numerical balancing requires iterative, hands-on tweaking rather than theoretical calculation.
- **Limits of Automated CI for "Game Feel"**: We assumed our CI pipeline and automated Quality Requirement Tests (QRTs) would catch all critical issues. While they perfectly caught syntax, linting, and structural errors, they could not catch subjective "game feel" issues (like the visual feedback of clicks or the satisfaction of the quota depletion). This validated that automated gates are essential for stability, but manual playtesting remains irreplaceable for user experience.

## Friction and gaps
- **Physics and Performance Friction**: Integrating the new 3D interactive elements (specifically the crowbar throwing mechanic and wooden plate destruction) introduced unexpected physics calculations that caused occasional frame drops in the Factory Room. We had to spend extra time optimizing collision layers and raycast distances to maintain a stable framerate.
- **Phase Logic Edge Cases**: Fixing the game logic so the "clicking phase" only initiates from direct player input revealed several edge cases in the state machine. Transitioning between the Preparation, Clicking, and Death phases required careful synchronization to prevent the timer from starting prematurely, which caused some friction during the final integration testing.
- **Subjective Quality Metrics**: A persistent gap in our quality assurance is the inability to automatically measure "fun" or "tension." While we have automated tests for performance (Time behaviour) and stability (Fault tolerance), we lack automated metrics for subjective player experience, leaving a gap that can only be bridged by human observation.

## Planned response
- **Demo Day Execution**: For the immediate next step, the team will shift entirely to presentation readiness. We will conduct timed rehearsals to ensure the 7-minute presentation and 7-minute Q&A fit perfectly within the limits, with smooth transitions between speakers and a flawless playback of the pre-recorded demo.
- **Finalizing Handover Status**: We will update `docs/customer-handover.md` to explicitly confirm the `Ready for independent use` handover level, ensuring all setup commands, known limitations (such as the manual balancing required for future factory tiers), and operational notes are clearly documented for the customer.
- **Future Technical Improvements**: If development were to continue post-course, we plan to investigate Godot's built-in performance profiling tools to create automated scripts that can detect physics bottlenecks in complex 3D rooms, bridging the gap between our automated CI and the game's runtime performance.
