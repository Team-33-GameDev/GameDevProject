# Week 7 Public Report: Sprint 5 (Final Maintenance & MVP v3 Delivery)

## Project Overview
**Click to Live** is a rogue-lite incremental clicker game developed in Godot 4. Set in a dystopian world, players must fulfill oppressive quotas by clicking under extreme time pressure, balancing high-stress clicking phases with relaxed preparation and meta-progression phases. 

This report covers **Sprint 5 (Week 7)**, focusing on follow-up maintenance based on the Week 6 customer trial, finalizing the product transition, delivering the final course version (**MVP v3**), and preparing for Demo Day.

### Remaining Limitations and Support Expectations
* **Limitations:** Spawning extreme quantities of Autoclickers (200+) may still cause minor frame drops on very low-end hardware due to Godot 4.2.2 3D particle rendering limits. This is documented in the handover guide with a recommended workaround (using the new visual cap toggle).
* **Support Expectations:** As this is a concluded university course project, the original development team is no longer providing active maintenance. Future bug fixes or feature requests should be managed by the customer or future maintainers via the GitHub repository, following the guidelines in `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

### Customer Independent Use Evidence
During the Week 7 transition confirmation meeting, the customer demonstrated that they had independently downloaded, installed, and played the build on their personal machine. They successfully completed a full game loop and shared a screenshot of their high score, confirming the product is fully operational on their side without team assistance.
