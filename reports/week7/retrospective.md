# Sprint 5 Retrospective (Week 7)

## What went well
- **Successful MVP v3 Delivery**: We successfully integrated the final gameplay adjustments (e.g., factory DPS rebalancing, phase logic fixes, and environmental interactions like the crowbar mechanics) and delivered a stable, polished `MVP v3` release.
- **Effective Handover Documentation**: The `docs/customer-handover.md` and root `README.md` were significantly improved based on Week 6 trial feedback, making the product much more accessible and understandable for independent use.
- **Demo Day Preparation**: The team coordinated effectively to divide presentation responsibilities, ensuring every member has a clear role and that the pre-recorded demo video is well-rehearsed and under the 2-minute limit.

## What did not go well
- **Final Customer Confirmation Friction**: Obtaining the final, explicit written confirmation of the handover status from the customer took longer than anticipated due to their limited availability during the final week. This created a slight bottleneck in finalizing the Week 7 Moodle submission evidence.
- **Last-Minute CI Polish**: A minor configuration oversight in the final release branch caused a brief CI failure related to linting on a newly added script, which required a hotfix PR right before the final tag was cut. 

## What the team changed or attempted to change based on the previous Sprint Retrospective, and what results they observed
- **Change**: In the Week 6 retrospective, we noted that customer feedback from the trial was sometimes discussed informally and not immediately traced to actionable PBIs. For Sprint 5, we enforced a strict rule: *every piece of feedback from the Week 6 trial must be logged as an issue or explicitly documented as "deferred" in the feedback response table before any code changes are made.*
- **Result**: This resulted in much cleaner traceability. All implemented changes in `MVP v3` (like the UI adjustments and gameplay balancing) were directly linked to specific customer feedback points, making the Sprint Review and final handover discussions highly evidence-based and efficient.
